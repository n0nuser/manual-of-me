Professional

My preferred channels of communication are...

In priority order:

Email

  • Preferably, I tend to forget things a lot.
  • It allows me to schedule time to respond to you properly

Instant messaging

  • Give me a bit of context for what you would like (check nohello.net)
  • If messages are urgent/time sensitive, it'd be good to know!

Video/Voice Chat

  • While I don't tend to be a fan of video calls, written communication can be prone to error and misinterpretation - if we're finding we aren't understanding well each other, let's move to a video/audio call to discuss things

I work best when

  • I can get my headphones on, have no distractions and get down to the task
  • Divide and Conquer tasks

What gives me energy at work

  • Having supportive colleagues
  • Being able to help grow myself and others, to improve ourselves technically and personally
  • Being able to have a good work/life balance
  • Having a joyful relaxed working atmosphere
  • Feeling valued

Things I need

  • Linux as the main OS. I even use it in my personal laptop and PC.
  • Ability to manage the PC I'm using
  • A bit fast computer, nothing special

Conditions I like to work in

  • Highly bright place with some plants and huge windows
  • I like having some white noise around me, but also like having my headphones on

I add value to a team by...

  • Being cheerful enough to make people smile
  • Critically thinking about the structure the work is organized in, and how to improve it continously. From refactoring code, to full automation of manual tasks.
  • Being a good communicator, and being able to explain complex things in a simple way
  • Thinking of software tools as what they are: tools. While I really like some frameworks as Django, I try not to attach to any tool: as long as it produces the expected product, it's fine!

Feedback is best given...

  • Nicely, please
  • Written, so I can refer back to it, and check how I'm performing
  • If we're discussing something sensitive, such as an interpersonal concern, I would like to discuss it in-person or with a call
  • I'd like to be aware of areas I need to improve, so I can work on them

Things I struggle with

  • I tend to forget things a lot: I need to note everything down and create events or task in a calendar so I can keep track of everything.
  • Dopamine. I usually find that after doing something that risens my dopamine, getting to other things not so pleasent is very difficult. This is normal for everyone because it's how the chemistry in our brains works, but I try to fight that by doing the hardest things the first.
  • Novelty-Seeking Behaviour. I tend to pursue new more interesting things while I already have some incredible things going around. While this might seem fine to many people, often it isn't. I'm working on my consistency and discipline.
